# Narrative Brief

## Premise

The town of Lumenwick survives by obeying an old rule: some things may travel only by moonlight. The player is a courier who moves those parcels through alleys, markets, bridges, and shrine roads before light exposes what is inside.

## Tone

- Mysterious but not grim.
- Cozy enough to invite replay.
- Strange enough to make every delivery feel slightly forbidden.

## Main Characters

| Character | Role | Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| The Courier | Player | Silent or lightly characterized protagonist |
| Mira Bellweather | Dispatcher | Teaches jobs, hints at town rules |
| Old Venn | Locksmith | Introduces shortcut keys and route secrets |
| Sister Lio | Shrine keeper | Connects packages to the town mystery |

## Story Arc

1. The courier learns that packages react to light.
2. The courier discovers each NPC is hiding why the deliveries matter.
3. The town's sealed routes begin changing at night.
4. Final delivery reveals that the parcels are memories being moved before dawn can erase them.

## Writing Rules

- Keep lines short and game-facing.
- Every delivery briefing must include destination, risk, and one flavorful mystery hook.
- Avoid lore dumps.
- Let package names and route details imply the world.
