# Moonlight Dispatch - Game Concept

## High Concept

**Moonlight Dispatch** is a 2D top-down route-planning, light-stealth, and small narrative game about a courier delivering packages that cannot be seen by daylight or patrol lamps. Each night is a compact 8-12 minute delivery puzzle: accept jobs, plan a route through a foggy folklore town, avoid light hazards, use one or two tools, deliver on time, and return before the moon sets.

## Commercial Pitch

Deliver strange parcels through a moonlit town where every shortcut has a price. Plan the route, dodge the lights, and learn why the packages must never be opened.

## Target Player

- PC indie players who enjoy short, tense sessions with clear goals.
- Fans of cozy-dark atmosphere, compact puzzles, and light narrative mystery.
- Players who like games such as route puzzles, small stealth sandboxes, and narrative delivery jobs, but do not want a large RPG or complex combat system.

## Business Positioning

- Platform: Windows and macOS.
- Channels: Steam first, itch.io parallel launch.
- Business model: paid premium game.
- Suggested launch price: USD 7.99.
- Suggested launch discount: USD 5.99.
- Payment setup: Steamworks and itch.io official payout flows, with Wise as a candidate receiving account where platform rules allow it.
- Session promise: one night is playable in 8-12 minutes.
- Full release promise: 60-90 minutes of authored story content plus repeatable daily route challenges.

## Player Fantasy

You are the only courier trusted to move moon-bound parcels through a town that punishes the careless. You survive by reading the city, finding quiet routes, and deciding which shortcuts are worth the risk.

## Design Pillars

1. **Readable Tension**
   - Every risk should be visible or foreshadowed.
   - Patrol light, timer pressure, and route blockage must be legible at a glance.

2. **Route Before Reflex**
   - The player wins primarily by planning and adapting, not by twitch execution.
   - Tools create route options rather than combat power.

3. **Small Town, Strong Mood**
   - The city is compact but dense with memory, rumors, and repeated landmarks.
   - Narrative is delivered through job notes, NPC lines, and package consequences.

4. **One More Night**
   - Each run is short enough to retry.
   - Score, timing, optional deliveries, and route variations encourage replay.

## MVP Scope

- One town map divided into four named districts.
- Twelve authored deliveries.
- Three risk families: patrol light, time pressure, route blockage.
- Four tools: Shade Cloak, Shortcut Key, Quiet Bell, Moonmark.
- Three NPC quest givers.
- One story arc with a clear ending.
- Daily Challenge mode with three generated-from-authored-rule deliveries.
- English UI first, Simplified Chinese localization as launch value-add.

## Out Of Scope

- Multiplayer.
- Mobile release.
- Ads or in-app purchases.
- Large-scale procedural city generation.
- Complex combat.
- RPG leveling trees.

## Success Criteria

- A player understands the commercial hook within 10 seconds of the store page.
- Three screenshots communicate courier, route risk, and delivery payoff.
- A 20-30 second trailer shows the full loop.
- The vertical slice produces a PROCEED verdict.
- The release candidate has no P0/P1 blockers and passes the final gate with PASS or PASS WITH CONCERNS.
